The Purdue Model / ISA-95

The Purdue Enterprise Reference Architecture (PERA) was developed by Theodore Williams at Purdue University in the early 1990s and formalized into the ISA-95 standard. It's the fundamental map of industrial systems.

Think of it as a security and functional boundary model — each level talks primarily to adjacent levels, not across the stack. This is why getting OT data to IT/cloud is non-trivial.

Level 5 │ Enterprise / Corporate IT (ERP: SAP, Oracle) Level 4 │ Site Business Planning / Logistics (MES, Historian aggregation) ─────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────────── DMZ / Level 3.5 Level 3 │ Site Operations / Manufacturing Ops (MES, LIMS, historians) Level 2 │ Area Supervisory Control (SCADA, DCS, HMI) ◄── THIS GUIDE Level 1 │ Basic Control (PLCs, RTUs, drives) ◄── THIS GUIDE Level 0 │ Physical Process (sensors, actuators) ◄── THIS GUIDE

Why This Matters Now

The industry is experiencing convergence pressure: cloud analytics platforms (Databricks, Azure, AWS) sit at Level 5, while the data is born at Level 0. Every byte of OT data must traverse 5 layers, each with its own protocols, vendors, and security posture.

Level 0 — The Physical Process

Level 0 is the physical world — atoms, electrons, heat, pressure, flow, position. Nothing here is digital by nature; it only becomes data when a transducer converts it.

Core function: Sense and actuate the physical process.

Signal Standards

4–20 mA
The workhorse of industry since the 1950s. 4 mA = 0%, 20 mA = 100%. The "live zero" at 4 mA distinguishes a failed sensor from a real zero reading.
HART Protocol
Digital overlay on 4–20 mA at 1200 baud. Two values for the price of one wire pair — analog plus digital diagnostics.
Ethernet-APL
The future: 2-wire Ethernet to field devices, intrinsically safe, 10 Mbit/s. Ratified 2021–2022, eliminates fieldbus translation.

Level 1 — Basic Control

Level 1 is where decisions happen in real time. Sensors from Level 0 feed in, logic executes (often in milliseconds), and commands go back to actuators.

Core function: Execute control logic, respond to process conditions, enforce safety limits.

Level 2 — Supervisory Control

Level 2 is where humans see and supervise the process. The SCADA system, DCS, and HMIs sit here, providing operators with real-time view and control.

Core function: Monitor, visualize, and supervisory-control the process. Log data. Raise alarms.

SCADA vs DCS

Aspect SCADA DCS
Geography Wide area (km to 1000s km) Single plant/site
Control emphasis Supervisory, not real-time Real-time tight control
Redundancy RTU-level System-wide, triple redundant
Integration Open, multi-vendor PLCs Proprietary, vendor-complete ecosystem
Typical industry Water, power transmission, pipelines Refining, chemicals, pharma, power gen
Typical I/O count Hundreds to thousands Thousands to 100,000+

Process Historians

The time-series database of Level 2. Stores all process values at high frequency with compression.

OSI PI (AVEVA)
Dominant globally, millions of installations. The de facto standard in oil & gas, energy, utilities. AVEVA paid $5B to acquire it in 2021.
Compression
Swinging-door compression: only store a point when the trend deviates from a straight line. Achieves 10–50:1 compression ratios.
Why They Exist
Standard databases can't handle 50,000 tags at 1-second intervals (50,000 rows/second). Process historians are purpose-built for this.

Key Protocols Reference

OPC-UA — The Convergence Protocol

Published 2006 by the OPC Foundation, OPC-UA is the most important OT protocol development in 20 years. Platform-independent, secure, semantic standard.

Information Model
Not just data values — full object model with types, methods, events
Security
X.509 certificates, session signing, encrypted transport (TLS)
Pub/Sub Extension
MQTT and AMQP transport — the bridge to cloud

MQTT and Sparkplug B

MQTT was created in 1999 for satellite monitoring of oil pipelines. Designed for constrained devices on unreliable networks.

Sparkplug B standardizes MQTT for industrial use with Google Protobuf payloads, birth/death certificates, and unified namespace concepts.

Current Trends (2023–2026)

1. IT/OT Convergence
OT data demanded by executive dashboards, AI/ML models, digital twins, ESG reporting. Architecture shifting from polling historians to streaming via MQTT/OPC-UA.
2. Unified Namespace
One MQTT broker as single source of truth. Most discussed architectural concept in IIoT 2022–2025.
3. Edge Computing
Local ML inference, data pre-processing before cloud transmission, offline operation when WAN drops.
4. Digital Twins
Real-time virtual replicas: descriptive (3D + sensors), diagnostic (root cause), predictive (what-if simulation).
5. AI/ML in OT
Predictive maintenance, process optimization, anomaly detection, computer vision for quality, early LLM adoption.
6. Ethernet-APL
2-wire Ethernet to field devices. The end of traditional fieldbuses for new installations (10–15 year rollout).
