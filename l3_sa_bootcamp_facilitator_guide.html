Overview & Learning Objectives

This interactive game teaches L3 Solution Architects (new hires fresh from training) how to navigate real customer scenarios using builder culture and AI-first principles.

Target Audience

  • L3 SAs: New graduates with strong technical backgrounds
  • Experience Level: Mostly no selling experience, but technical expertise
  • Skill Gap: Need practice in discovery, objection handling, competitive positioning, and translating technical capability to business value

2026 Learning Priorities

Builder Culture: SAs must BUILD custom solutions WITH customers, not present canned demos
AI-First: Use Genie, AI Assistant, AutoML, AI/BI to accelerate everything (70% AI-generated, 30% human)

Learning Outcomes

  1. Ask the right discovery questions before positioning solutions
  2. Design customer-specific architectures (not generic reference slides)
  3. Build working prototypes in hours using AI tools
  4. Handle competitive objections with honesty and credibility
  5. Manage POC scope and timelines
  6. Build CFO-grade business cases with honest TCO analysis
  7. Understand the SA role as a builder, not just a presenter

Game Setup

Before the Session

  1. Open the game: L3 SA Bootcamp Game
  2. Screen share: Share your screen with the full game visible
  3. Test audio/video: Ensure everyone can see and hear
  4. Decide on teams: 3-6 teams of 3-5 people each works best
  5. Explain format: "This is a team-based scenario game. I'll unlock each case, you'll discuss and answer, I'll reveal hints/answers and award points."

Team Setup

  1. Click "Begin the Quest" on the intro screen
  2. Add team names (use company names, favorite customers, or fun team names)
  3. Click "Launch Game" to start
Important: The game is designed for YOU (the facilitator) to control. Participants should NOT have the game open on their own screens — they watch your screen share.

Facilitator Controls

Main Controls (Bottom of Each Case)

  • 🔓 Unlock Scenario: Reveals the case to teams (starts locked)
  • 💡 Give Hint: Shows a hint to help teams if they're stuck
  • ✅ Reveal Answer: Shows the full answer and learning points
  • ⚔️ Next Scenario: Moves to the next case (or finishes the game)

Scoring Controls (Left Sidebar)

  • +1, +2, +3 buttons: Award points for good answers
  • -1 button: Deduct points (use sparingly, usually for jokes)
  • Top team: Highlighted with gold border as scores update

Typical Flow for Each Case

  1. Unlock the case — Teams read the scenario
  2. Give teams 2-5 minutes to discuss in breakout rooms or as full group
  3. Call on teams to share their answers
  4. Award points as they answer (+1 for partial, +2 for good, +3 for excellent)
  5. Give hint if teams are stuck or missing key insights
  6. Reveal answer when discussion is complete
  7. Debrief — Emphasize builder culture and AI-first principles
  8. Next scenario — Move forward

Scenario Breakdown

Case 1 — Discovery & Qualification
Learning Goal: Ask the right questions before positioning. Builder culture = build a working notebook after the call.

Scenario: First discovery call with a retail company. Vague requirements ("wants AI"). 60 minutes to prepare.

Key Learning: Discovery is about uncovering business pain, not pitching features. After the call, BUILD something (don't just send a deck).

Scoring Guide:
+1: Asks about pain points or timeline
+2: Asks about AI use case specifics AND mentions building a prototype
+3: Full discovery framework + builder culture move (send working notebook within 24 hours)
⏱️ Suggested Time: 8-10 minutes total
Case 2 — Solution Architecture
Learning Goal: Design customer-specific architectures. Builder culture = build a working example after whiteboarding.

Scenario: Healthcare company. CISO asks about HIPAA compliance, data governance, and PHI isolation.

Key Learning: Start with customer constraints (CISO security concerns), not generic reference architecture. After the call, BUILD a governed pipeline demo.

Scoring Guide:
+1: Mentions Unity Catalog or governance
+2: Addresses CISO concerns + operational simplicity
+3: Full architecture approach + builder culture (build working Unity Catalog demo with AI Assistant)
⏱️ Suggested Time: 10-12 minutes total
Case 3 — Builder Culture
Learning Goal: Build custom solutions in days using AI-first tools. Don't show canned demos.

Scenario: Logistics company wants delay prediction. You have 4 days to build a working prototype from their S3 data.

Key Learning: Use AI Assistant (70% code generation), AutoML (no manual model training), and AI/BI (fast dashboards). Build in hours, not weeks.

Scoring Guide:
+1: Has a build plan
+2: Mentions using AI tools (AutoML, AI Assistant)
+3: Full 4-day plan with AI-first acceleration + working prototype delivery
⏱️ Suggested Time: 12-15 minutes total (EMPHASIZE THIS ONE)
Case 4 — Competitive Positioning
Learning Goal: Answer Snowflake objections with honesty and credibility. Don't trash-talk competitors.

Scenario: Customer asks 4 technical questions comparing Databricks to Snowflake (zero-copy cloning, SQL vs Spark, data sharing, positioning).

Key Learning: Be honest about Snowflake strengths. Answer with technical truth, not marketing spin. Credibility > winning every point.

Scoring Guide:
+1: Answers 1-2 questions correctly
+2: Answers all 4 + acknowledges Snowflake is a good product
+3: Full answers + identifies "Databricks is for data science" as the landmine question
⏱️ Suggested Time: 10-12 minutes total
Case 5 — Technical Objections
Learning Goal: Respond to security/compliance objections with technical precision. Know when to escalate.

Scenario: CISO sends 5 security concerns as blockers (data residency, secrets management, network isolation, SSO, audit logging).

Key Learning: Security is a checklist, not a negotiation. Know what's easy (SSO, secrets) vs what needs architecture discussion (Private Link) vs what requires escalation (control plane residency).

Scoring Guide:
+1: Identifies easy solves (SSO, audit logging)
+2: Knows Private Link is the answer for network isolation
+3: Full triage + knows when to escalate data residency question
⏱️ Suggested Time: 10-12 minutes total
Case 6 — POC Management
Learning Goal: Manage scope creep. Builder culture = prototype fast with AI, don't extend timelines.

Scenario: Customer asks for 4 new POC items on Day 8 of a 2-week POC (migrate 40 DAGs, AutoML demo, performance tuning, extend to 4 weeks).

Key Learning: Use AI tools to compress 3 days of work into 3 hours. Say YES by building prototypes, not by extending scope. AutoML trains models in 1 hour, AI Assistant converts DAGs in 1 hour.

Scoring Guide:
+1: Knows to push back on DAG migration
+2: Identifies performance tuning as critical + offers to build AutoML demo
+3: Full AI-first strategy (build prototypes in hours, don't extend timeline)
⏱️ Suggested Time: 10-12 minutes total
Case 7 — TCO & Business Case
Learning Goal: Build honest ROI cases. Don't overpromise savings. CFOs value credibility over optimism.

Scenario: CFO asks for business case justification. Databricks is $420K vs Snowflake $310K. Needs Year 1 cost avoidance, productivity gains, revenue impact, and TCO comparison.

Key Learning: Year 1 is investment, not savings. Don't claim revenue impact you can't validate. Be honest if Snowflake is cheaper for SQL-only use cases.

Scoring Guide:
+1: Identifies cost avoidance opportunities
+2: Knows Year 1 is investment + quantifies productivity gains
+3: Full honest TCO analysis + scopes revenue impact to technical enablement only
⏱️ Suggested Time: 10-12 minutes total
Case 8 — The Final Riddle
Learning Goal: Understand the SA role in the builder culture + AI-first era.

Scenario: A riddle describing the SA job.

Key Learning: SAs are builders who sell by showing, not telling. AI-first is the default mode (Genie, AI Assistant, AutoML are co-pilots).

Scoring Guide:
+1: Guesses "Solution Architect"
+2: Adds "builder culture"
+3: Full answer including AI-first principles
⏱️ Suggested Time: 5-8 minutes total

Scoring Guidelines

Point System

  • +1 point: Partial answer, shows understanding of basic concept
  • +2 points: Good answer, covers most key points
  • +3 points: Excellent answer, includes builder culture or AI-first insights
  • -1 point: Use sparingly (usually for humor, not wrong answers)

When to Award Points

Be generous with points! The goal is learning, not competition. Award points for:
  • Good discussion, even if answer isn't perfect
  • Mentioning builder culture ("I would BUILD this...")
  • Mentioning AI-first tools (Genie, AI Assistant, AutoML, AI/BI)
  • Honest answers (e.g., "I don't know, I'd escalate to SE manager")
  • Creative solutions

Common Mistakes to Correct

  • "I'd send them a deck" → Redirect: "What would you BUILD instead?"
  • "I'd spend 3 days coding this" → Redirect: "What AI tools would accelerate this?"
  • "Snowflake is bad because..." → Redirect: "How do you answer honestly without trash-talking?"
  • "I'd extend the POC to 4 weeks" → Redirect: "How do you compress scope, not extend time?"

Facilitation Tips

Creating Good Discussion

  1. Use breakout rooms: Give teams 2-3 minutes to discuss before sharing
  2. Call on specific teams: "Team Snowflake, what would you do?"
  3. Ask follow-up questions: "Why is that important?" "What tool would you use?"
  4. Encourage debate: "Team AWS disagrees — what's your take?"
  5. Connect to real experience: "Has anyone faced this in real life?"

Emphasizing Builder Culture

After EVERY scenario, ask: "What would you BUILD to prove this?"

Examples:
  • Discovery → Build a working notebook with their schema
  • Architecture → Build a governed pipeline demo
  • POC scope → Build AutoML model in 1 hour instead of 3 days
  • Competitive → Build side-by-side shallow clone demo

Emphasizing AI-First

After EVERY scenario, ask: "What AI tools would accelerate this?"

Examples:
  • Genie → Search internal docs for similar use cases
  • AI Assistant → Generate 70% of code from schema/requirements
  • AutoML → Train models in 1 hour, not 3 days
  • AI/BI → Build dashboards 10x faster

Handling Quiet Teams

  • Direct questions: "Team Delta Lake, I haven't heard from you yet — what's your take?"
  • Lower the stakes: "There's no wrong answer here, what's your first instinct?"
  • Offer hints earlier: Don't let teams struggle for 5+ minutes
  • Share your experience: "When I was an L3, I made this mistake..."

Handling Dominant Teams

  • Rotate who answers: "Team Unity Catalog already answered last time, let's hear from Team Photon"
  • Ask for different perspectives: "Good answer. Who has a different approach?"
  • Award bonus points for quiet teams: "+1 bonus for first answer from Team Delta"

Time Management

Session Length Options

Full Game (8 scenarios): 90-120 minutes
Short Version (5 scenarios): 60 minutes (Cases 1, 3, 4, 6, 8)
Lightning Round (3 scenarios): 30 minutes (Cases 1, 3, 8)

Per-Scenario Time Budget

  • Read scenario: 1 minute (facilitator reads aloud or teams read silently)
  • Team discussion: 2-5 minutes (depends on complexity)
  • Teams share: 3-5 minutes (2-3 teams answer)
  • Reveal answer + debrief: 2-3 minutes
  • Total per scenario: 8-14 minutes

Staying on Schedule

  1. Set time limits upfront: "We have 10 minutes for this case"
  2. Use a visible timer: Share timer on screen or use phone
  3. Cut discussion if running long: "Great discussion, let's move to the answer"
  4. Skip hints if teams nail it: Go straight to reveal answer
  5. Prioritize debrief over team answers: The learning is more important than hearing every team

Which Scenarios to Prioritize

If you need to skip scenarios, keep these:

  1. Case 1 (Discovery): Foundational skill
  2. Case 3 (Builder Culture): MOST IMPORTANT — this is the 2026 priority
  3. Case 4 (Competitive): They'll face Snowflake in 90% of deals
  4. Case 6 (POC Management): Practical skill they'll use immediately
  5. Case 8 (Final Riddle): Good capstone, reinforces builder + AI-first identity
