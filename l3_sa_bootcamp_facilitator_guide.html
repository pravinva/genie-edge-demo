Overview

SE pairs work through Meridian Health Systems across four rounds (Q1–Q4). Each round: read Events, discuss diagnosis offline, then each pair chooses their top three actions from that quarter’s lettered menu. After teams debrief aloud, you reveal GM ground truth from the source scenario bank — not from the app.

Learning objectives (from scenario bank)

  • Diagnose account-health signals and likely causes behind consumption changes
  • Prioritize actions under partial information and limited time
  • Know when to engage cross-functional partners (SSA, Technical Services, Support) vs when not to escalate
  • Shift mindset from “save the account” to “grow the account” as the story evolves
Scoring in the app: 3 points for each selected action that matches the optimal set for that round (max 9 per round, 36 total). After pairs report their three letters, enter them under each team in the sidebar and click Apply points for this team. The app shows how many points were recorded — not which letters were “supposed” to be correct.

Setup

  1. Open l3_sa_bootcamp.html?gm=1 on the facilitator / projector machine (Apps Script or GitHub Pages — add ?gm=1).
  2. Click Begin session → add a row per competing pair (only named rows count; use + Add team for extras) → Launch simulator.
  3. Explain: diagnosis is offline; the app only shows events + action menu; you will reveal ground truth verbally after each round.
Screen share: Everyone can watch the main board. Scoring: Only the facilitator (or co-facilitator) should click letter chips and Apply points so scores stay accurate. Participants can keep notes on paper — they do not need their own copy of the app unless you choose that format.
Two links (important):
• Facilitator / projector: open the same HTML file with ?gm=1 on the URL (example: …/l3_sa_bootcamp.html?gm=1). That unlocks Unlock round, Advance round, the scoreboard, and Apply points.
• Participants (optional): share the URL without ?gm=1. They only see the intro handoff text — they cannot advance rounds or see scores. Most sessions use projector only and do not give participants a link.
Re-scoring a team: If you change letter picks for the same round and click Apply points again, the total adjusts (it replaces this round’s points for that team — it does not stack duplicate +3s).

Controls & flow

  • Unlock this round — Reveals events, diagnosis instructions, and the action table.
  • Sidebar — For each team, tap exactly three action codes they committed to, then Apply points for this team. Points = 3 × (how many of their picks match the optimal set for that round). Wrong picks are ignored (no negative points).
  • Lock round → next quarter — After debrief and scoring, advance. On Q4, the button reads Finish → standings.

Round-by-round facilitation

Q1 — The First Cracks

Focus discussion on connecting signals (champion exit, SQL drop, Tom’s benchmarks). After teams share their three actions, reveal ground truth from the PDF, then score in the app.

⏱️ Suggested: 12–18 min
Q2 — The Plot Thickens

New VP from a Snowflake-heavy background, ML up / SQL down, UC ticket storm. Good tension: discovery vs pitch, when TS is warranted, and when ML SSA is premature.

⏱️ Suggested: 12–18 min · Optional: 2-min brainstorm of discovery questions for David
Q3 — Turning Point

TS fixed UC; David wants serving demo + cost review; Tom’s proposal lands. Debrief influence without blocking customer process and credible numbers vs vendor TCO decks.

⏱️ Suggested: 12–18 min
Q4 — The Close

Account has turned; choices shift to EBR + joint success plan + SQL Serverless POC. Contrast with “celebrate only” or over-rotating to TS migration too early.

⏱️ Suggested: 12–18 min

Scoring reference

  • Per pick: +3 if the letter is one of the three optimal actions for that round.
  • Cap: 9 points per round per team; 36 overall.
  • Ties: Allowed — standings screen orders by total.

Discussion prompts (from scenario bank)

Use these after teams share their three actions and you reveal ground truth:

  • Diagnosis: “Who connected champion loss to the consumption story? Walk us through that reasoning.”
  • Partial information: “Did anyone treat these as three separate problems vs one arc?”
  • Actions: “Pair A pulled in an SSA early; Pair B did not — both can be argued. Make your case.”
  • Risk: “What could go wrong with the highest-leverage move you chose?”
  • Escalation judgment: “Was this a round where the best move was not to escalate? Who got that?”
  • Discovery (Q2): “You have 30 minutes with David — top three questions, no product names.”
  • Reflection: “What signal from this quarter would you watch on your own accounts Monday?”

Time management

Full run (4 rounds): about 55–80 minutes (12–18 min per round + transitions).
Tight agenda: shorten pair discussion to ~5 min and debrief to ~5 min per round (~45 min total).
  • Read events aloud (optional) while the slide is unlocked — 1–2 min.
  • Pair work — diagnosis + agree on three letters — 5–8 min.
  • Share-outs — a few teams report — 4–6 min.
  • GM reveal + debate — use PDF narrative — 5–8 min.
  • Enter scores in sidebar — 1–2 min.

Checklist

Before: Simulator opens · teams decided · PDF / answer key ready · timer optional
During: Unlock → discuss → share → verbal reveal → enter three letters per team → Apply points → next round
After: Show standings screen · recap themes · collect feedback

Deploying (Apps Script)

  1. Copy the contents of l3_sa_bootcamp.html into an HtmlService file (e.g. Index.html) in your existing web app project, or serve it via HtmlService.createHtmlOutputFromFile / template as you already do for ?mode=l3bootcamp.
  2. Redeploy the web app so the new HTML is live at your script.google.com/.../exec URL.
  3. Keep l3_sa_bootcamp_facilitator_guide.html alongside it if you link facilitators to the guide from the same folder/host.
